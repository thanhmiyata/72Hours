The Wake-Up Call West Coast Families Are Finally Hearing

Living on the West Coast means living with earthquakes. In Japan, disaster kits are common household items. Here, many moms quietly plan "figure it out later".
 But experts like the Red Cross and FEMA warn that every household needs its own 72hours emergency kit . When the ground shakes, you won't have time to run to the store. You'll need safe water, ready-to-eat food, reliable light, warmth, and first aid already at the door. Keeping those critical supplies on hand gives you peace of mind that your family can manage on its own for at least the first three days after a disaster.
Know The RISK
Some emergencies are seasonal, others can strike without warning. From earthquakes on the West Coast to ice storms in the East, every region of Canada has unique risks. Knowing your zone helps you prepare the right way.

The 10‑Minute Reality Check

Having these items ready can make all the difference in the first critical minutes after a quake

Water

Taps break fast
1 gallon per person per day is essential.

Power & Light

Outages happen instantly. Hands-free light keeps you safe.

Communication

Phones jam. A crank radio delivers lifesaving updates.

Food & Energy

Stress burns calories. Ready-to-eat meals keep you moving.

Cold & Warmth

Nights get cold. One blanket or bag per person is critical.
