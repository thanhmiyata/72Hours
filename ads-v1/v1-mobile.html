The Wake-Up Call West Coast Families Are Finally Hearing
Living on the West Coast means living with earthquakes. In Japan, disaster kits are common household items. Here, many moms quietly plan “figure it out later”.
But experts like the Red Cross and FEMA warn that every household needs its own 72‑hour emergency kit. When the ground shakes, you won’t have time to run to the store. You’ll need safe water, ready-to-eat food, reliable light, warmth, and first aid already at the door. Keeping those critical supplies on hand gives you peace of mind that your family can manage on its own for at least the first three days after a disaster.

The 10‑Minute Reality Check
Having these items ready can make all the difference in the first critical minutes after a quake:
Water
Taps break fast — 1 gallon per person per day is essential.
Power & Light
Outages happen instantly. Hands-free light keeps you safe.
Communication
Phones jam. A crank radio delivers lifesaving updates.
Food & Energy
Stress burns calories. Ready-to-eat meals keep you moving.
Cold & Warmth
Nights get cold. One blanket or bag per person is critical.

Day 3 🚑 Help usually arrives - but only if you’ve made it through.

A Simple Solution: A Ready-Made 72-Hour Kit
For busy families, a convenient answer is a pre-packed emergency kit. The 72HRS 1‑Person Deluxe Kit, for example, bundles all the essentials for one person for 72 hours. It typically includes sealed water pouches, high-calorie meal bars, a flashlight, first-aid items, a hand-crank NOAA radio, warm blankets, and more exactly the items FEMA’s checklist recommends. Having a kit like this at the ready means you’ve covered FEMA’s 3-day guideline with quality gear. In an emergency, you can simply grab the kit.
